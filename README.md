I teach machines and people how to think about data. My work sits at the intersection of data science, public policy, and education — guided by a consistent philosophy: **the why behind the how.**

### What I Do

At **Georgetown University**, I teach [PPOL 5206: Massive Data Fundamentals](https://github.com/joskog/ppol-5206) to Master's students in the Data Science for Public Policy program, covering SQL, cloud computing, distributed systems, and big data technologies.

I run the **[Data Liberation Project](https://www.data-liberation-project.org/)**, filing FOIA requests to make government datasets publicly available.

Through **Delta Lima LLC** and **Patowmack Consulting**, I help organizations build data infrastructure, governance frameworks, and analytics capabilities — with experience spanning federal agencies (PCAOB, Census Bureau, Fannie Mae) and private sector clients.

On **Data Craft**, I'm building a library of data education content that treats learners as thinkers, not just practitioners. I also volunteer with the **U.S. Coast Guard Auxiliary**.
